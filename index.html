About me.

My experience in psychotherapy has taken me to government and private in- and out-patient settings, addiction facilities and bereavement counselling supervision. In these many encounters with people looking to make life different, I have witnessed the importance of the wish for change and a good therapeutic relationship in undertaking this journey.

Therapy

Starting therapy can feel like a daunting step. A mentor of mine once said that we have courage when we feel that we aren’t alone. By having a space for you (or you and your partner or family) to explore the difficulties you might be facing, you will hopefully be able to draw on the courage to work through different challenges. Finding a good therapy space can make all the difference to how you see and respond to your life, and to how you negotiate your relationships, which so often determine the quality of one’s life.

Interests

My training was in psychodynamic psychotherapy which assists people in gaining insight into various aspects of their lives. I tend to integrate techniques from Dialectical Behaviour Therapy, Schema Therapy and Mindfulness. Schema therapy, in particular, is helpful in recognising behavioural patterns and coping mechanisms that cause more difficulty in the long-run and in learning how to identify and heal these old habits.
